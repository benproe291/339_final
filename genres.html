Overview of Common EDM Subgenres

EDM or Electronic Dance Music is a collection of genres that has evolved from eachother and other genres of music. EDM is the broad term to describe music that anything electronic, however people who often listen to any kind of EDM usually have a particular subgenre they favor other the others.
Below are a few artists that represent their subgenre.

Hover over an image below to display the image and the alt text.

Dubstep

Artist Pictured: Subtronics

The overall sound of a dubstep track is described as "tightly coiled productions with overwhelming bass lines and reverberant drum patterns, clipped samples, and occasional vocals."

Click to visit www.AllMusic.com for more info about Dubstep

Tech House

Artist Pictured: Fred again..

The overall sound of a tech house track is described as "a mixing style, tech-house often brings together deep or minimal techno music, the soulful and jazzy end of house, some minimal and very often some dub elements."

Click to visit Wikipedia.com for more info about Tech House

Deep House

Artist Pictured: ZHU

The overall sound of a deep house track is described as "Deep house is known for tempos typically from 110 to 125 bpm,muted basslines, spacious use of percussion elements, soft keyboard sounds (pads), use of advanced chord structures, ambient mixes, and soulful vocals. Lyrics usually focus on positive/uplifting themes or more melancholic subjects akin to blues."

Click to visit Wikipedia.com for more info about Deep House

Trap

Artist Pictured: RL Grime

The overall sound of a EDM Trap genre is described as "[an evolving] genre that is evolving has seen the use of techno, dub, and dutch house like sounds incorporated with the inclusion of the original Roland TR-808 drum samples and vocal samples used by the originators."

Click to visit runthetrap.com for more info about EDM Trap

Future Bass

Artist Pictured: San Holo

The overall sound of a Future Bass genre is described as "a style of electronic dance music which developed in the 2010s that mixes elements of dubstep and trap with warmer, less abrasive rhythms."

Click to visit Wikipedia.com for more info abuot Future Bass

Drum & Bass

Artist Pictured: High Contrast

The overall sound of a Drum & Bass genre is described "by fast breakbeats with heavy bass and sub-bass lines, samples, and synthesizers."

Click to visit Wikipedia.com for more info about Drum & Bass

Mid Tempo Bass

Artist Pictured: REZZ

The overall sound of a Mid Tempo Bass genre is described as "a derivation of the Techno and Elector House evolving to have a driving 4-on-the-floor beat, abrasive basses, glitchy fills and dark atmospheres."

Click to visit edm.fansom.com for more info Midtempo Bass

Melodic Bass/Melodic Dubstep

Artist Pictured: Said the Sky

The overall sound of a Melodic Bass genre is described as "blend of forceful sonics and dreamy atmospheres, with side-chained walls of low-end wreathed in sweet, airy toplines."

Click to visit rateyourmusic.com for more info about Melodic Bass

Tropical House

Artist Pictured: Lost Frequencies

The overall sound of the Tropical House genre is described as "having a more uplifting and relaxing sound. The tempo of tropical house songs is a little slower than deep house (100-115 bpm). Tropical house does not use the pumping compression effect of "big room" electro house."

Click to visit Wikipedia.com for more info about Tropical House
